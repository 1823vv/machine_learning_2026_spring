## Question: Weight Penalty and Neural Network Capacity

A neural network with very large weights can fit highly complex patterns but may overfit noisy training data. Weight penalties constrain the effective capacity of the model to improve generalization.

1. Explain how a weight penalty restrains a neural network from overfitting.
2. Why does the training loss typically increase after adding a regularization penalty?
3. Explain why the validation loss can decrease even when the training loss increases.
4. Draw a single diagram showing training and validation loss curves over epochs *without* regularization, and a second diagram showing the curves *with* regularization.
5. What happens to the network's effective capacity if the regularization strength is made extremely large? Describe the resulting effect on both training and validation performance.
