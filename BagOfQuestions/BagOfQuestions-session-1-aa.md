## Question: MAE versus MSE Intuition

For regression, let the prediction error for one example be $e=\hat y-y$. Mean absolute error uses the magnitude of the error, while mean squared error squares the error before averaging.

1. Write the formulas for MAE and MSE over $n$ examples, using double pipes for absolute values.
2. Draw or describe a figure comparing $\|e\|$ and $e^2$ as functions of the error $e$.
3. Explain why MSE punishes one large error more strongly than MAE.
4. Give one reason MSE is convenient for gradient descent.
